Internet trends and memes explained for seniors


You may have realised that there are a range of trends that people are talking about that you don't understand or you have no idea what they are, well you are not alone so we have compiled a list of words and some videos here that might help explain what they are, or maybe they will just confuse you further! If you have any questions of new trends why not email us and let us know and we will try our best to figure it out.

An Internet meme may take the form of an image, hyperlink, video, picture, website, or hashtag. It may be just a word or phrase, including an intentional misspelling. These small movements tend to spread from person to person via social networks, blogs, direct email, or news sources. They may relate to various existing Internet cultures or subcultures, often treated or spread on sites such as 4chan, Reddit and numerous others.


Planking


This really is what it sounds like, people will lie like a plank and take a picture or video of it. The trick here is people may do this in a variety of dangerous or weird places.



From Planking a few other weird trends or spin offs have occurred. Such as "owling", which involves usually climbing to a high space and squatting, while looking into the distance, much like an owl. Another spin-off is bat-manning, which involves hanging upside down in odd spaces and taking pictures or videos.



Milking


This involves filming yourself showering yourself in milk, usually in a public place. That's it. 



Taking pictures of people taking pictures with iPads


Yes this exists and in fact a website exists purely for pictures of people taking pictures with iPads.



Dyeing your pet


This involves using dye to make your pet, usually a dog , look like another animal, often a tiger lion, or panda, and in some cases has resulted in the police being called, to chase a lion loose in the suburbs .



Cat Beard


This involves holding a cat to your face, so that it looks like a beard.



Baguetting


This involves using a baguette in place of an everyday object and taking a photo.



Flash Mob


A flash mob is where people seemingly part of an everyday crowd or activity, start singing or joining in a song, seemingly randomly.




Viral Advertising


This is where companies, create or make a scenario, usually filming unsuspecting members of the public and then release the video to the public, in the hope that many people will share the video, thus creating free advertising for the company.



Harlem Shake


This is a dance move that has recently enjoyed a revival, it is sort of a dance shake.



Twerking


Is a dance move, where the per on shakes, wobbles and jiggles usually their bottom and hips, in a sexual movement.
